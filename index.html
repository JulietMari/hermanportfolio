Project 01

2D: Uncanny Valley

By Juliet Herman

My plan for this project was to show the Hawaiian identity that would forever be changed following the Bombing of Pearl Harbor. Hawaii is such a beautiful location known for its relaxed, positive and welcoming spirit. On December 7th of 1941, this beautiful spirit would be tainted as Oahu’s Pearl Harbor was bombed in a surprise attack from Japan that would ultimately cause the United States to enter into WWII.

In my piece, I wanted to portray the typical Hawaiian beauty but with an edgy undertone revealing a new tainted side of the state. I used a shadow of the tropical image which I later filled in with cutouts from the newspaper. I additionally embedded the eyes from the novel cover in the tropical image to help portray that sense of beauty with an adjacent edgier version of the image embedded in the roots of the shadowy tree to show the shattered beauty that the bombing would leave.

For this assignment–-to creatively explore a 3D object-–I wanted to be sure I selected something that was close to home. Music has always been a huge, important part of my life so naturally, I chose to model a guitar to represent this creative side of me that makes me feel powerful, certain and proud. However, a beautiful aspect of music in my opinion is the fact that it’s never perfect. The raw honesty that musicians include in their pieces is truly what makes them so meaningful. For this reason, I decided to alter my guitar just a bit so it wouldn’t be quite so perfect and instead have some much-needed character. I decided to make it so the strings of the guitar have snapped-–the reason for this is up for interpretation…perhaps because the musician was just too passionate.
